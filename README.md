# CS 250 Portfolio Reflection – SNHU Travel Project

## 1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How do “user stories” help with this?

Interpreting user needs starts with understanding what the user is trying to accomplish rather than just focusing on technical requirements. During the SNHU Travel project, user stories helped break down these needs into clear and simple goals. Each user story followed the format: "As a user, I want to do something so that I can achieve a goal." This made it easier to understand the purpose behind each feature.

For example, when working on the top five destinations feature, the user story helped guide what needed to be included, such as images, descriptions, and links. Instead of guessing what to build, the user story clearly defined expectations. This allowed development to stay aligned with user needs and ensured that the final product provided real value.

## 2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach developing programs by breaking large tasks into smaller, manageable pieces and focusing on completing them step by step. During this project, the Agile approach helped structure my workflow through short sprints, which allowed for continuous progress and improvement.

In the future, I plan to continue using Agile practices such as:
- Sprint planning to organize work into smaller tasks
- Daily stand-ups (or check-ins) to track progress
- Iterative development to improve features over time
- Retrospectives to reflect and improve performance

These practices help keep development flexible and allow quick adjustments when requirements change. Instead of trying to build everything at once, Agile focuses on delivering working features continuously.

## 3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member means contributing to both the technical work and the overall team environment. Communication is one of the most important parts of being effective in a team. During the project, sharing updates, discussing blockers, and collaborating with others helped prevent issues and improve efficiency.

A good team member also takes responsibility for their work while being willing to support others when needed. This includes writing clear code, following requirements, and helping maintain progress toward team goals. In Agile teams, collaboration is especially important because everyone contributes to delivering a working product.

Being reliable, communicative, and adaptable are key qualities of a strong team member in software development.
